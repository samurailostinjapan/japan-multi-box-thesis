# 6. Corporate Japan and SMEs

Corporate Japan is not one sector.

Large exporters, trading houses, megabanks, semiconductor-equipment firms, and companies with foreign earnings can benefit from a weak yen or higher rates.

Domestic SMEs often face the opposite conditions: higher imported inputs, weak household demand, limited pricing power, higher financing costs, labor shortages, and shrinking regional markets.

Large corporate cash balances do not automatically produce investment.

Cash can be defensive: debt service, inventory, imported materials, maintenance, payroll, and liquidity protection.

This is the **cash-hoard capex paradox**.

Higher rates may therefore increase the value of holding cash while simultaneously raising the hurdle rate for new investment.

The stress path is likely to appear through delayed capex, slower hiring, smaller bonuses, supplier pressure, business closures, and regional-bank credit losses.

AI, semiconductors, and industrial investment can produce real winners. The question is whether those gains spread broadly enough to repair domestic wages, services, and tax capacity.

# 11. Future Projections

The thesis uses scenarios rather than a single deterministic forecast.

## 2026–2027

Expected features:

- further BOJ normalization;
- rate pain spreading through firms and households;
- continued FX intervention;
- persistent weak-yen pressure;
- more selective capex;
- early bonus and hiring weakness;
- continued household support.

## 2028–2030

This is the main convergence window.

Expected features:

- visibly higher debt service;
- permanent or semi-permanent benefit commitments;
- greater care spending;
- weaker domestic absorption;
- tighter BOJ room;
- more political sensitivity to household pain;
- a more visible childbearing-age cohort constraint.

## 2030–2035

The issue becomes operating degradation: regional hospitals, care services, transport, SMEs, municipal finance, regional banks, and labor supply.

## 2035–2045

Physical capacity becomes as important as money.

Japan must manage fewer caregivers, more elderly living alone, stronger competition for foreign workers, weaker regional capacity, higher reliance on targeted support, and more market-sensitive borrowing.

## Base case

Japan remains functional, borrows successfully, supports strategic industries, and avoids sudden crisis. The yen remains structurally weak, household surplus declines, and policy becomes more targeted and expensive.

## Upside case

Productivity gains from AI, automation, energy, and advanced manufacturing spread broadly enough to raise wages, tax capacity, and domestic investment.

## Downside case

An energy shock, disorderly yen move, JGB credibility break, corporate investment decline, and political relief package arrive together.
